7.1. Academic Integrity with LLMs#

Disclosure Requirements:

  • Many journals now require disclosure of AI use

  • Some prohibit AI-generated text without disclosure

  • Check your target journal/conference guidelines

  • When in doubt, disclose

Example Disclosure Statement:

“AI language models (Claude 3.5 and GPT-4) were used to assist with literature synthesis and code documentation. All AI-generated content was verified by the authors, and citations were checked for accuracy.”

Authorship Questions:

  • LLMs cannot be listed as authors (per major publishers)

  • AI assistance should be disclosed in acknowledgments or methods

  • Original intellectual contribution must be human

Data Privacy Concerns:

  1. Don’t upload confidential data to public LLM services

  2. Use institutional accounts when available (data protection)

  3. Anonymize data before uploading for analysis

  4. Check terms of service - some LLMs may use inputs for training

Bias and Fairness:

  • LLMs can perpetuate biases from training data

  • Be especially cautious with research involving:

    • Human subjects

    • Marginalized populations

    • Politically sensitive topics

    • Medical/health applications

Best Practices Summary:

  1. Use LLMs as assistants, not replacements for expertise

  2. Always verify factual claims and citations

  3. Disclose AI use appropriately

  4. Maintain critical evaluation of outputs

  5. Protect confidential/sensitive data

  6. Understand limitations and failure modes

  7. Keep humans in the loop for critical decisions
