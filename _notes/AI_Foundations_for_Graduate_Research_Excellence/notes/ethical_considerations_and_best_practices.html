7. Part 7: Ethical Considerations and Best Practices#

7.1. Academic Integrity with LLMs#

Disclosure Requirements:

  • Many journals now require disclosure of AI use

  • Some prohibit AI-generated text without disclosure

  • Check your target journal/conference guidelines

  • When in doubt, disclose

Example Disclosure Statement:

"AI language models (Claude 3.5 and GPT-4) were used to assist with literature synthesis and code documentation. All AI-generated content was verified by the authors, and citations were checked for accuracy."

Authorship Questions:

  • LLMs cannot be listed as authors (per major publishers)

  • AI assistance should be disclosed in acknowledgments or methods

  • Original intellectual contribution must be human

Data Privacy Concerns:

  1. Don’t upload confidential data to public LLM services

  2. Use institutional accounts when available (data protection)

  3. Anonymize data before uploading for analysis

  4. Check terms of service - some LLMs may use inputs for training

Bias and Fairness:

  • LLMs can perpetuate biases from training data

  • Be especially cautious with research involving:

    • Human subjects

    • Marginalized populations

    • Politically sensitive topics

    • Medical/health applications

Best Practices Summary:

  1. ✓ Use LLMs as assistants, not replacements for expertise

  2. ✓ Always verify factual claims and citations

  3. ✓ Disclose AI use appropriately

  4. ✓ Maintain critical evaluation of outputs

  5. ✓ Protect confidential/sensitive data

  6. ✓ Understand limitations and failure modes

  7. ✓ Keep humans in the loop for critical decisions

8. Part 8: Recap and Key Takeaways (5 minutes)#

8.1. Core Concepts Review#

About LLMs:

  1. LLMs predict next tokens based on probability, not understanding

  2. Context windows have hard limits - design accordingly

  3. More parameters ≈ better reasoning, but at cost of resources

  4. Quantization allows powerful models to run on modest hardware

About Prompting:

  1. Structured frameworks (AIM, ERA, CARE, ROSES) dramatically improve output

  2. Specificity matters - vague prompts produce vague results

  3. Chain-of-thought prompting enhances complex reasoning

  4. Few-shot examples teach through pattern demonstration

  5. Context and constraints guide model behavior

About Research Applications:

  1. LLMs can’t search literature - you must provide sources

  2. Always verify citations and factual claims

  3. Use specialized tools for domain-specific tasks

  4. Combine LLMs with traditional research methods

  5. Maintain critical evaluation throughout

Common Pitfalls to Avoid:

  • ❌ Trusting citations without verification

  • ❌ Exceeding context window limits without chunking strategy

  • ❌ Using vague prompts and expecting specific results

  • ❌ Forgetting to specify output format and constraints

  • ❌ Uploading confidential data to public services

  • ❌ Skipping disclosure of AI use

Action Items for Students:

Immediate (This Week):

  1. Create an account with 2-3 LLM platforms (free tiers available)

  2. Try the AIM framework on one real research task

  3. Experiment with chain-of-thought prompting

  4. Check your institution’s AI use policy

Short-term (This Month):

  1. Develop prompt templates for recurring tasks

  2. Build a library of effective few-shot examples

  3. Explore specialized research tools (Consensus, Elicit)

  4. Practice verification workflows

Ongoing:

  1. Stay updated on new models and capabilities

  2. Join research communities discussing AI tools

  3. Share effective prompts with peers

  4. Maintain critical evaluation skills
