5.1. Mini-Lab: Apply What You’ve Learned (20 minutes)#

Step 1: Identify YOUR Research Task (2 min)

Write down ONE concrete research task you need help with in the next 30-60 days:

  • Clarifying a research question

  • Drafting survey items or interview questions

  • Structuring an outline from messy notes

  • Generating pseudocode or documentation

  • Synthesizing literature findings

  • Analyzing qualitative data themes

  • Other: __________

Step 2: Choose Your Track (15 min)

TRACK 1 – Foundations (Newer to AI):

  1. Write a naive prompt for your task

  2. Run it in your preferred LLM (ChatGPT, Claude, Copilot, etc.)

  3. Critique the output: What’s missing or generic?

  4. Rewrite using AIM or ERA framework + at least one principle:

    • Role definition

    • Constraints (length, format)

    • Examples (few-shot)

  5. Run again and compare outputs

  6. Document: What improved? What still needs work?

TRACK 2 – Advanced (Already Use LLMs Regularly):

  1. Pick your research task

  2. Incorporate TWO advanced techniques:

    • Chain-of-thought reasoning (“think step by step”)

    • Few-shot examples (provide 2-3 samples)

    • Explicit evaluation criteria (OCEAN-style)

    • Verification requirements

  3. Design and run prompt

  4. Refine based on output

  5. Document: What techniques had biggest impact?

Step 3: Cross-Share (3 min)

One volunteer from each track shares:

  • The research task

  • The approach used

  • One key insight about what worked (or didn’t)

Debrief Questions:

  • What surprised you most about the LLM’s capabilities?

  • What limitations did you encounter?

  • How will you use this in your actual research?

  • What additional skills do you want to develop?
