3. Part 3: Advanced Prompting Techniques#

3.1. Chain-of-Thought (CoT) Prompting#

Building on basic prompting fundamentals, Chain-of-Thought techniques unlock more sophisticated reasoning capabilities.

Definition

Encouraging step-by-step reasoning by telling the LLM to “think aloud” and show its work [Wang et al., 2022].

Research Foundation

Chain-of-Thought prompting was introduced by Google researchers in 2022 and has been shown to significantly improve performance on complex reasoning tasks, with gains of 20-50% on arithmetic and logical reasoning benchmarks [Wei et al., 2022].

Basic Technique:

"Let's break this down step by step. First, [identify what], then [analyze how], finally [conclude]."

Example 3.1 (Research Question Refinement with CoT)

Prompt:

I'm interested in studying "social media effects on mental health."

Help me refine this into a focused research question by thinking through:

Step 1: Identify what's too vague (which social media? which mental health outcomes? which population?)

Step 2: Narrow to one platform, one outcome, one demographic

Step 3: Add a time dimension or comparative element

Step 4: Propose 3 refined research questions ranked by feasibility for a master's thesis

Show your reasoning at each step.

Why This Works:

  • Forces systematic decomposition of complex problem

  • Allows you to evaluate reasoning quality, not just final answer

  • Catches logical errors in intermediate steps

  • Makes model more likely to consider multiple factors

Example 3.2 (Code Generation with CoT)

Prompt:

I need Python code to clean a dataset with the following issues: missing values (represent as NaN), duplicate rows, and outliers beyond 3 standard deviations.

Think through this step by step:
1. First, import necessary libraries and explain why each is needed
2. Then, handle missing values - show the code and explain your approach
3. Next, remove duplicates - explain what constitutes a duplicate
4. Finally, handle outliers - explain the statistical reasoning
5. Provide the complete, executable code with comments

Show your reasoning at each step before writing the final code.

3.1.1. Advanced CoT Variations#

Different research scenarios call for different CoT approaches.

1. Zero-Shot CoT

Simply add “Let’s think step by step” to any prompt. Surprisingly effective with no examples needed.

2. Few-Shot CoT

Provide 2-3 examples with reasoning steps shown:

Example 1: "The capital of France is Paris because France's administrative center has been in Paris since the medieval period."

Example 2: "The capital of Japan is Tokyo because Tokyo became the capital in 1868 when the emperor moved from Kyoto."

Now answer: What is the capital of Brazil?
3. Contrastive CoT

Show both correct and incorrect reasoning:

✓ Correct: "Ottawa is the capital of Canada because it was chosen as a political compromise between Montreal and Toronto."

✗ Incorrect: "Toronto is the capital of Canada because it's the largest city" [This is wrong because capital cities aren't always the largest cities]

Now answer: What is the capital of Australia?

3.2. Few-Shot Learning: The Power of Examples#

When explicit step-by-step reasoning isn’t enough, showing examples can dramatically improve output quality.

Definition

Providing 2-5 input-output examples to teach the model desired behavior through pattern recognition [Brown et al., 2020].

Why It Works

LLMs excel at pattern matching. When given examples, they infer the underlying task structure and apply it to new inputs.

Example 3.3 (Paper Classification for Literature Reviews)

Prompt:

You are a research librarian helping with literature reviews.

I'm writing a literature review on remote work and productivity. Learn from these examples:

Relevant: "The impact of remote work on employee productivity during 2020-2023"
Reasoning: Directly addresses remote work and productivity with recent timeframe.

Not Relevant: "Office design and workspace productivity"
Reasoning: Focuses on physical office spaces, not remote work specifically.

Relevant: "Work-from-home policies and performance outcomes in tech companies"
Reasoning: Directly examines remote work policies and their impact on performance.

Not Relevant: "Productivity software tools for project management"
Reasoning: About tools rather than remote work arrangements.

Now classify this title: "Telecommuting and job satisfaction: A survey of remote workers"

Is this relevant or not relevant to my review? Give your reasoning.

Expected Output:

Relevant

Reasoning: This paper directly examines telecommuting (remote work) and its effects on workers. While the outcome measured is job satisfaction rather than productivity, job satisfaction is closely related to productivity and would provide important context for understanding remote work effectiveness. The survey methodology also offers empirical data from actual remote workers, which would complement other studies in the literature review.

Best Practices for Few-Shot Examples

  1. Diversity: Examples should cover different edge cases

  2. Quality: Use correct, high-quality examples

  3. Quantity: 2-5 examples optimal (diminishing returns after 5) [Min et al., 2022]

  4. Clarity: Make examples crystal clear with explicit labels

  5. Relevance: Examples should closely match target task

3.3. The OCEAN Framework: Sharpening Output Quality#

For comprehensive, high-stakes prompts, the OCEAN framework integrates multiple best practices into a cohesive structure [theMITmonk, 2023].

3.3.1. Components#

  1. O - Objective: What precisely do you want to achieve?

    "I need to identify methodological limitations in a qualitative study."

  2. C - Context: What background information is relevant?

    "This is a phenomenological study using semi-structured interviews with 12 participants examining lived experiences of remote work during COVID-19."

  3. E - Examples: Show what good output looks like.

    "Example limitation: 'Small sample size (n=12) limits transferability to broader populations, particularly given the unique context of pandemic-era remote work.'"

  4. A - Action: What specific steps should be taken?

    "Read the methodology section, identify 3-5 limitations, explain why each is significant, and suggest how future research could address each."

  5. N - Needs: What format and constraints apply?

    "Provide as numbered list, 50-75 words per limitation, using technical terminology appropriate for publication."

Example 3.5 (Complete OCEAN Framework Application)

Objective: Identify methodological limitations in this qualitative research paper to strengthen my literature review critique.

Context: This is a phenomenological study using semi-structured interviews with 12 participants examining lived experiences of remote work during COVID-19. The study uses thematic analysis as its analytical approach.

Examples: 
Example limitation: "Small sample size (n=12) limits transferability to broader populations, particularly given the unique context of pandemic-era remote work."

Example limitation: "Reliance on self-reported data may be subject to recall bias and social desirability effects, particularly regarding productivity claims."

Action: 
1. Read the methodology section carefully
2. Identify 3-5 significant methodological limitations
3. For each limitation, explain: (a) what it is, (b) why it matters for this study, (c) how it affects interpretation
4. Suggest one way future research could address each limitation

Needs: 
- Provide as numbered list
- 50-75 words per limitation
- Use technical terminology appropriate for graduate-level research methods
- Focus on limitations that genuinely affect the study's validity or transferability, not trivial issues
