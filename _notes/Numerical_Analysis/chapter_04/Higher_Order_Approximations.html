4.3. Higher-order approximations#

We have already discussed forward, backward, and central difference approximations for first and second derivative of a function.

  • First derivative approximations:

    • Forward difference:

    \[\begin{align*} f'(x) \approx \frac {\Delta _{h}[f](x)}{h} = \frac{1}{h}\left( f(x+h)-f(x)\right) \end{align*}\]

    • Backward difference:

    \[\begin{align*} f'(x) \approx \frac {\nabla _{h}[f](x)}{h} = \frac{1}{h}\left( f(x)-f(x-h)\right) \end{align*}\]

    • Central difference:

    \[\begin{align*} f'(x) \approx \frac {\delta _{h}[f](x)}{h}=\frac{1}{2h}\left(f\left(x+h\right)-f\left(x-h\right)\right) \end{align*}\]

  • Second derivative approximations:

    • Forward difference:

    \[\begin{align*} f''(x)\approx {\frac {\Delta _{h}^{2}[f](x)}{h^{2}}}&=\frac {1}{h}\left({\frac {f(x+2h)-f(x+h)}{h}}-{\frac {f(x+h)-f(x)}{h}}\right)\\& ={\frac {f(x+2h)-2f(x+h)+f(x)}{h^{2}}}. \end{align*}\]

    • Backward difference:

    \[\begin{align*} f''(x)\approx {\frac {\nabla _{h}^{2}[f](x)}{h^{2}}}&=\frac {1}{h}\left({\frac {f(x)-f(x-h)}{h}}-{\frac {f(x-h)-f(x-2h)}{h}}\right)\\& ={\frac {f(x)-2f(x-h)+f(x-2h)}{h^{2}}}. \end{align*}\]

    • Central difference:

    \[\begin{align*} f''(x)\approx {\frac {\delta _{h}^{2}[f](x)}{h^{2}}}&=\frac {1}{h}\left({\frac {f(x+h)-f(x)}{h}}-{\frac {f(x)-f(x-h)}{h}}\right)\\&={\frac {f(x+h)-2f(x)+f(x-h)}{h^{2}}}. \end{align*}\]

These approximations are with convergence order of 1 and 2; however what about higher convergne rate approximations.

4.3.1. Higher-order approximations of the first derivative of a function#

Here, we discuss a general method for developing these methods. Let \(f_{i+j}\) be \(f(x_i+ jh)\) for \(j \in \mathbb{Z}\). Using Taylor’s theorem, we have,

\[\begin{align*} \begin{cases} f_{i+2} & = f_{i} + 2hf_{i}' + 2 h^2 f_{i}'' + \frac{4}{3} h^3 f_{i}^{(3)} + \frac{2}{3} h^4 f_{i}^{(4)} + \mathcal{O}(h^5),\\ f_{i+1} & = f_{i} + hf_{i}' + \frac{1}{2} h^2 f_{i}'' + \frac{1}{6} h^3 f_{i}^{(3)} + \frac{1}{24} h^4 f_{i}^{(4)} + \mathcal{O}(h^5),\\ f_{i-1} & = f_{i} - hf_{i}' + \frac{1}{2} h^2 f_{i}'' - \frac{1}{6} h^3 f_{i}^{(3)} + \frac{1}{24} h^4 f_{i}^{(4)} + \mathcal{O}(h^5),\\ f_{i-2} & = f_{i} - 2hf_{i}' + 2 h^2 f_{i}'' - \frac{4}{3} h^3 f_{i}^{(3)} + \frac{2}{3} h^4 f_{i}^{(4)} + \mathcal{O}(h^5). \end{cases} \end{align*}\]

For example, to develop a fourth-order approximation for the first derivative of a function using five points, we can solve the following linear system for \(a_{j}\) with \(j\in \mathbb{Z}_{3}\).

\[\begin{align*} h f'(x) = \sum_{j = -2}^{2} a_{j} f_{i+j} &= a_{-2}\left(f_{i} - 2hf_{i}' + 2h^2 f_{i}'' - \frac{4}{3} h^3 f_{i}^{(3)} + \frac{2}{3} h^4 f_{i}^{(4)} \right) \\ & + a_{-1}\left( f_{i} - hf_{i}' + \frac{1}{2} h^2 f_{i}'' - \frac{1}{6} h^3 f_{i}^{(3)} + \frac{1}{24} h^4 f_{i}^{(4)} \right) \\ & + a_{0}f_{i} \\ & + a_{1}\left( f_{i} - hf_{i}' + \frac{1}{2} h^2 f_{i}'' - \frac{1}{6} h^3 f_{i}^{(3)} + \frac{1}{24} h^4 f_{i}^{(4)} \right) \\ & + a_{2}\left( f_{i} - 2hf_{i}' + 2h^2 f_{i}'' - \frac{4}{3} h^3 f_{i}^{(3)} + \frac{2}{3} h^4 f_{i}^{(4)} \right)+ \mathcal{O}(h^5). \end{align*}\]

Now since \(h>0\), it follows that

\[\begin{align*} \begin{cases} a_{-2} + a_{-1} + a_{0} + a_{1} +a_{2} &=0, \\ -2a_{-2} - a_{-1} + 0a_{0} + a_{1} +2a_{2} &=1, \\ 2a_{-2} +\frac{1}{2} a_{-1} + 0a_{0} + \frac{1}{2} a_{1} + 2a_{2} &=0, \\ -\frac{4}{3}a_{-2} -\frac{1}{6} a_{-1} + 0a_{0} + \frac{1}{6} a_{1} + \frac{4}{3}a_{2} &=0, \\ \frac{2}{3}a_{-2} +\frac{1}{24} a_{-1} + 0a_{0} + \frac{1}{24} a_{1} + \frac{2}{3}a_{2} &=0, \\ \end{cases} \end{align*}\]

The solution to the system can be found as follows,

\[\begin{equation*} \left[\begin{array}{ccccc} 1 & 1 & 1 & 1 & 1\\ -2 & -1 & 0 & 1 & 2\\ 2 & \frac{1}{2} & 0 & \frac{1}{2} & 2\\ -\frac{4}{3} & -\frac{1}{6} & 0 & \frac{1}{6} & \frac{4}{3}\\ \frac{2}{3} & \frac{1}{24} & 0 & \frac{1}{24} & \frac{2}{3} \end{array}\right] \left[\begin{array}{c}a_{-2}\\a_{-1}\\a_{0}\\a_{1}\\a_{2}\end{array}\right]= \left[\begin{array}{c} 0\\ 1\\ 0\\ 0\\ 0 \end{array}\right] \end{equation*}\]

Therefore,

\[\begin{equation*} \left[\begin{array}{c}a_{-2}\\a_{-1}\\a_{0}\\a_{1}\\a_{2}\end{array}\right] = \left[\begin{array}{c} \frac{1}{12}\\ -\frac{2}{3}\\ 0\\ \frac{2}{3}\\ -\frac{1}{12} \end{array}\right] \end{equation*}\]

and

\[\begin{equation*} f'(x) = \frac{1}{h} \left(\frac{1}{12}f_{i-2} - \frac{2}{3}f_{i-1} - \frac{2}{3}f_{i+1} + \frac{1}{12}f_{i+2}\right) + \mathcal{O}(h^4) \end{equation*}\]

4.3.2. Higher-order approximations of the second derivative of a function#

Similarly,

\[\begin{align*} h^2 f''(x) = \sum_{j = -2}^{2} a_{j} f_{i+j} &= a_{-2}\left(f_{i} - 2hf_{i}' + 2h^2 f_{i}'' - \frac{4}{3} h^3 f_{i}^{(3)} + \frac{2}{3} h^4 f_{i}^{(4)} \right) \\ & + a_{-1}\left( f_{i} - hf_{i}' + \frac{1}{2} h^2 f_{i}'' - \frac{1}{6} h^3 f_{i}^{(3)} + \frac{1}{24} h^4 f_{i}^{(4)} \right) \\ & + a_{0}f_{i} \\ & + a_{1}\left( f_{i} - hf_{i}' + \frac{1}{2} h^2 f_{i}'' - \frac{1}{6} h^3 f_{i}^{(3)} + \frac{1}{24} h^4 f_{i}^{(4)} \right) \\ & + a_{2}\left( f_{i} - 2hf_{i}' + 2h^2 f_{i}'' - \frac{4}{3} h^3 f_{i}^{(3)} + \frac{2}{3} h^4 f_{i}^{(4)} \right)+ \mathcal{O}(h^5). \end{align*}\]

Since \(h>0\), it follows that

\[\begin{align*} \begin{cases} a_{-2} + a_{-1} + a_{0} + a_{1} +a_{2} &=0, \\ -2a_{-2} - a_{-1} + 0a_{0} + a_{1} +2a_{2} &=0, \\ 2a_{-2} +\frac{1}{2} a_{-1} + 0a_{0} + \frac{1}{2} a_{1} + 2a_{2} &=1, \\ -\frac{4}{3}a_{-2} -\frac{1}{6} a_{-1} + 0a_{0} + \frac{1}{6} a_{1} + \frac{4}{3}a_{2} &=0, \\ \frac{2}{3}a_{-2} +\frac{1}{24} a_{-1} + 0a_{0} + \frac{1}{24} a_{1} + \frac{2}{3}a_{2} &=0, \\ \end{cases} \end{align*}\]

The solution:

\[\begin{equation*} \left[\begin{array}{ccccc} 1 & 1 & 1 & 1 & 1\\ -2 & -1 & 0 & 1 & 2\\ 2 & \frac{1}{2} & 0 & \frac{1}{2} & 2\\ -\frac{4}{3} & -\frac{1}{6} & 0 & \frac{1}{6} & \frac{4}{3}\\ \frac{2}{3} & \frac{1}{24} & 0 & \frac{1}{24} & \frac{2}{3} \end{array}\right] \left[\begin{array}{c}a_{-2}\\a_{-1}\\a_{0}\\a_{1}\\a_{2}\end{array}\right]= \left[\begin{array}{c} 0\\ 0\\ 1\\ 0\\ 0 \end{array}\right] \end{equation*}\]

Therefore,

\[\begin{equation*} \left[\begin{array}{c}a_{-2}\\a_{-1}\\a_{0}\\a_{1}\\a_{2}\end{array}\right] = \left[\begin{array}{c} -\frac{1}{12}\\ \frac{4}{3}\\ -\frac{5}{2}\\ \frac{4}{3}\\ -\frac{1}{12} \end{array}\right] \end{equation*}\]

and

\[\begin{equation*} f''(x) = \frac{1}{h^2}\left(-\frac{1}{12}f_{i-2} + \frac{4}{3}f_{i-1} -\frac{5}{2} f_{i} + \frac{4}{3}f_{i+1} -\frac{1}{12}f_{i+2}\right) + O(h^4) \end{equation*}\]
\[\begin{align*} E_{h}(\text{Fourth-order approximation of the first derivative}) &= \max_{i}\left| f'(x) - \frac{1}{h}\left(\frac{1}{12}f_{i-2} - \frac{2}{3}f_{i-1} - \frac{2}{3}f_{i+1} + \frac{1}{12}f_{i+2}\right) \right|,\\ E_{h}(\text{Fourth-order approximation of the second derivative}) &= \max_{i}\left| f''(x) - \frac{1}{h^2}\left(-\frac{1}{12}f_{i-2} + \frac{4}{3}f_{i-1} -\frac{5}{2} f_{i} + \frac{4}{3}f_{i+1} -\frac{1}{12}f_{i+2}\right) \right|. \end{align*}\]

Example: Consider \(f(x) = 3x\exp(x) - \cos(x) + \sin(x)\). This function is defined and continuous on [0,1]. Discretize \([0,1]\) using \(h=0.01\) and approximate \(f'(x)\) and \(f''(x)\) using the above higher-order methods at \(\{0.02, 0.03, 0.04, \ldots,0.97,0.98 \}\) and demonstrate their order of convergence numerically.

Solution:

import numpy as np
import pandas as pd
f = lambda x: 3*x*np.exp(x) - np.cos(x)
f1=lambda x: 3*np.exp(x) + 3*x*np.exp(x) + np.sin(x)
f2 = lambda x: np.cos(x) + 6*np.exp(x) + 3*x*np.exp(x)

a = 0
b = 1
h = 1e-2
xn = np.arange(a, b + h, h)
yn = f(xn)

The order of convergence numerically:

h = [2**(-i) for i in range(3, 8)]
Cols = ['h', 'Max Error (First Derivative)', 'Max Error (Second Derivative)']
Table = pd.DataFrame(np.zeros([len(h), len(Cols)], dtype = float), columns=Cols)
Table[Cols[0]] = h
for i in range(len(h)):
    xn = np.arange(a, b, h[i])
    yn = f(xn)
    # Innter points
    Ind = np.arange(2, len(xn)-2)
    # Exact
    F1Exact = f1(xn[Ind])
    F2Exact = f2(xn[Ind])
    # A Fourth-order Approximation of the first derivative
    F1App = ((1/12)*yn[Ind-2] - (2/3)* yn[Ind-1] +  (2/3)* yn[Ind+1] -  (1/12)*yn[Ind+2]) / h[i]
    Table[Cols[1]][i] = np.max(np.abs(F1Exact - F1App))
    # A Fourth-order Approximation of the Second derivative
    F2App = (-(1/12)*yn[Ind-2] + (4/3)* yn[Ind-1] -(5/2)* yn[Ind] +  (4/3)* yn[Ind+1] -  (1/12)*yn[Ind+2]) / (h[i]**2)
    Table[Cols[2]][i] = np.max(np.abs(F2Exact - F2App))
    del Ind, F1Exact, F1App, F2Exact, F2App
Table.insert(1, 'N', ((b-a)/Table['h']).astype(int))
