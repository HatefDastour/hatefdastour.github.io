<p style='text-align: justify;'>
This course provides students with a comprehensive exploration of descriptive statistics, probability theory, and systematic approaches to inferential statistics. The curriculum encompasses a wide range of fundamental topics, such as making inferences about population means and proportions, comprehending regression and correlation, conducting chi-square tests, analyzing variance, and utilizing non-parametric statistics. Additionally, students will have valuable hands-on experience applying these statistical methods to real-world problems in the fields of biological and health sciences.
</p>
